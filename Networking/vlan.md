# VLAN (Virtual Local Area Network)

## 📚 Introduction

A Virtual Local Area Network (VLAN) is a logical grouping of devices within a network that allows network segmentation without requiring separate physical networks.

VLANs improve security, reduce broadcast traffic and simplify network management.

---

## 🔑 Why VLANs are Important

VLANs help:

- Segment networks logically
- Improve security
- Reduce broadcast traffic
- Simplify administration
- Enhance network performance

---

## 🌐 How VLANs Work

Devices connected to the same VLAN can communicate directly.

Devices in different VLANs require a router or Layer 3 switch for communication.

---

## 📋 VLAN IDs

Each VLAN is identified by a VLAN ID.

Examples:

- VLAN 10 – Sales Department
- VLAN 20 – HR Department
- VLAN 30 – IT Department

---

## 🔌 Types of Ports

### Access Port

Connects end devices and belongs to a single VLAN.

### Trunk Port

Carries traffic for multiple VLANs between switches.

---

## 🔄 Inter-VLAN Communication

Communication between different VLANs requires:

- Router-on-a-Stick
- Layer 3 Switch

---

## 🛡 Advantages of VLANs

- Better security
- Reduced broadcast domains
- Improved network efficiency
- Easier network management

---

## 🌍 Real-World Applications

VLANs are commonly used in:

- Enterprise networks
- Universities
- Data centers
- Corporate environments

---

## 🔑 Key Takeaways

- VLAN stands for Virtual Local Area Network.
- VLANs provide logical network segmentation.
- Access ports connect end devices.
- Trunk ports carry traffic for multiple VLANs.
- VLANs improve security and performance.
